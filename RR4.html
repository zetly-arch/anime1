2.Itachi Uchiha

Itachi Uchiha is a fictional character from the anime and manga series Naruto, created by Masashi Kishimoto. He is a member of the Uchiha clan, known for their Sharingan eye technique, and is initially introduced as an antagonist. Itachi is portrayed as a highly skilled ninja with a tragic backstory, having killed his entire clan except for his younger brother, Sasuke Uchiha. This act sets off a chain of events that drives the plot of the series, as Sasuke seeks revenge against Itachi.


Itachi's character is complex, as he is revealed to have acted out of a desire to protect his brother and the village of Konoha from greater threats. He is known for his calm demeanor, intelligence, and mastery of various jutsu, including the powerful Tsukuyomi genjutsu. Itachi's story explores themes of sacrifice, loyalty, and the burdens of being a shinobi. His character has become iconic within the Naruto franchise, and he is often cited as one of the series' most beloved characters due to his depth and the moral dilemmas he faces.


Despite his tragic actions, Itachi is ultimately portrayed as a hero who made difficult choices for the greater good. His legacy continues to influence the series long after his death, as his actions and ideals resonate with other characters, particularly Sasuke. He was remebered as a clan killer and a hero who sacrificed everything for his brother and village. And his story serves as a poignant reminder of the complexities of duty, love, and the consequences of one's choices in the world of shinobi. During the time he spent in the Akatsuki organization, no member dared to challenge him, and he was known for his stoic and enigmatic presence and no one dared to attack on Konoha when he was alive and he was considered one of the strongest shinobi of his time almost on par with the Copy Ninja Kakashi Hatake.


Despite getting a lot of hate for killing his clan and being a member of the Akatsuki, Itachi's true intentions were revealed later in the series, showcasing his deep love for his brother and his village. At last he reaveled that he was a double agent and his actions were meant to protect Konoha from the threat of the Akatsuki and other enemies. And his death was a turning point for Sasuke, who ultimately comes to understand Itachi's sacrifices and the true nature of his brother's love.
